Katsushika Hokusai

The Unconventional Master of Japanese Art

Unique traits of the master:

  • Katsushika Hokusai’s exact date of birth is unknown, although he's thought to have been born on 30 October 1760
  • Hokusai began painting as a child, and to quote his own words "From the time I was six, I was in the habit of sketching things I saw around me."
  • Hokusai had become an apprentice wood carver and at 14 was accepted as apprentice by the ukiyo-e and woodcarver master Katsukawa Shunsho.
  • He was known by at least 30 different names during his lifetime, which was a record even for the artistic habit of that time.
  • His most famous series is Thirty-six views of Mount Fuji, which includes his most iconic work, which inclused his famous The Great Wave of Kanagawa.
  • Hokusai had a talent for self-promotion, a skill that, combined with his unique artistic skills, helped hm getting the attention of Shogun Tokugawa Ienari.
  • He made more than 30,000 works during his lifetime, a number experts agreen on depsite his studio was destroyed by a fire in 1839.
  • He wasn’t afraid of growing old and actually looked at aging as the natural process thay qould have allowed him to reach his artistic maturity and final mastery.
  • He never ceased to innovate his style, an attituded that allowed hom to realize some of his most remarkable creations when he was in his 80s.

"If I could only have one more day, I could do a great painting. If heaven gives me ten more years, or an extension of even five years, I shall surely become a true artist."

-- Hokusai
